### 1.3 Intended use {#13-intended-use}

Please read this manual and all accompanying documents in their entirety before commissioning the laser module.

If in doubt or if you need additional information, please contact us before using the module.

#### 1.3.1 Normal Use {#131-normal-use}

The PLH3D-XT-50 laser module integrated into MEKANIKA CNC mills is intended for:

<ul style="list-style-type: none;">
  <li><i class="fas fa-square-check" style="color: #4EBD7D;"></i> Laser engraving on wood and wood-based materials</li>
  <li><i class="fas fa-square-check" style="color: #4EBD7D;"></i> Laser engraving on leather</li>
  <li><i class="fas fa-square-check" style="color: #4EBD7D;"></i> Laser engraving on textiles</li>
  <li><i class="fas fa-square-check" style="color: #4EBD7D;"></i> Laser engraving on compatible plastics (e.g. opaque acrylic)</li>
  <li><i class="fas fa-square-check" style="color: #4EBD7D;"></i> Marking on anodized metals (e.g. anodized aluminum)</li>
  <li><i class="fas fa-square-check" style="color: #4EBD7D;"></i> Marking on stainless steel and titanium</li>
  <li><i class="fas fa-square-check" style="color: #4EBD7D;"></i> Cutting thin compatible materials (mainly wood and wood-based materials < 5 mm) through multiple passes</li>
</ul>

#### 1.3.2 Prohibited uses {#132-prohibited-uses}

The following uses are **PROHIBITED**:

<ul style="list-style-type: none;">
  <li><i class="fas fa-xmark" style="color: #d32f2f;"></i> Engraving or cutting PVC (releases toxic chlorine)</li>
  <li><i class="fas fa-xmark" style="color: #d32f2f;"></i> Engraving or cutting polycarbonate (releases toxic fumes)</li>
  <li><i class="fas fa-xmark" style="color: #d32f2f;"></i> Working on materials containing asbestos</li>
  <li><i class="fas fa-xmark" style="color: #d32f2f;"></i> Use in explosive atmospheres</li>
  <li><i class="fas fa-xmark" style="color: #d32f2f;"></i> Outdoor use or in humid environments</li>
  <li><i class="fas fa-xmark" style="color: #d32f2f;"></i> Use without laser safety glasses</li>
  <li><i class="fas fa-xmark" style="color: #d32f2f;"></i> Use without adequate ventilation</li>
  <li><i class="fas fa-xmark" style="color: #d32f2f;"></i> Working on reflective materials without tilting</li>
</ul>

## 2. LASER SAFETY {#2-laser-safety}

> **<i class="fas fa-ban" style="color: #d32f2f;"></i> DANGER - CLASS 4 LASER**
>
> **This laser module is classified as Class 4 according to EN 60825-1. It represents a major risk to eyes and skin. Direct exposure or diffuse reflection to the laser beam can cause permanent eye damage and severe skin burns.**

Read the entire operating manual to familiarize yourself with the laser module's features and how to use them. Improper operation can result in serious bodily harm, including permanent eye damage, burns and fire risk.

**It is imperative to follow the safety instructions listed in this manual at all times.**

### 2.1 Laser classification {#21-laser-classification}

| **PARAMETER** | **VALUE**                   |
| :------------ | :-------------------------- |
| Laser class   | Class 4 (without enclosure) |
| Wavelength    | 445 ± 10 nm (blue)          |
| Optical power | 6 W                         |
| Beam type     | Continuous                  |

**Class 4 meaning:**

- The direct beam presents a danger to eyes and skin
- Diffuse reflections also present a danger
- Fire risk when exposing flammable materials

### 2.2 Safety instructions by risk level {#22-safety-instructions-by-risk-level}

The following terms are used in this manual to indicate different levels of potential danger when using the laser module.

> **<i class="fas fa-ban" style="color: #d32f2f;"></i> DANGER** - _Instructions which, if not properly followed, create a probability of serious bodily harm, permanent eye injury, severe burns or death._

| **RISK TYPE**              | **SAFETY INSTRUCTIONS**                                                                                                                                                                                                             |
| :------------------------- | :---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Direct laser radiation** | Never look directly at the laser beam. Exposure of the eyes to the direct beam or reflected by specular reflection can cause permanent and irreversible eye damage. Always wear OD7+ laser safety glasses for the 190-540 nm range. |
| **Diffuse reflection**     | Avoid any exposure to diffuse reflections of the laser beam. Even reflections on non-polished surfaces can be dangerous.                                                                                                            |
| **Skin burns**             | Exposure of skin to the laser beam can cause severe burns. Wear tight-fitting long-sleeved clothing and avoid any skin exposure.                                                                                                    |
| **Fire risk**              | The laser beam can ignite combustible materials. Never use the laser in the presence of flammable liquids, gases or dust. Keep a fire extinguisher nearby. The work area must be properly ventilated.                               |
| **Toxic fumes and vapors** | Laser engraving and cutting produce potentially toxic fumes. Ensure adequate ventilation of the work area. Never work on PVC or other plastic/composite materials that release toxic vapors.                                        |

> **<i class="fas fa-exclamation-triangle" style="color: #ff9800;"></i> RISK** - _Instructions which, if not properly followed, create a probability of property damage and a possibility of injury._

| **RISK TYPE**   | **SAFETY INSTRUCTIONS**                                                                                                                        |
| :-------------- | :--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Clothing**    | Dress appropriately. Do not wear loose clothing that could be ignited. Wear dark clothing that absorbs laser light better than light clothing. |
| **Work area**   | Access to the laser work area must be restricted to authorized personnel only. Install laser warning signs at entrances.                       |
| **Supervision** | Never leave the machine running unattended when the laser is active.                                                                           |

> **<i class="fas fa-info-circle" style="color: #2196f3;"></i> CAUTION** - _Instructions which, if not properly followed, create a possibility of minor injury and property damage._

| **RISK TYPE**   | **SAFETY INSTRUCTIONS**                                                                                                                                                                             |
| :-------------- | :-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Maintenance** | Before any maintenance operation, turn off the laser and disconnect the power supply. Wait for the laser to cool before handling.                                                                   |
| **Lens**        | The laser front lens must be cleaned regularly using a lint-free cloth or optical paper, lightly dampened with isopropyl alcohol (≥ 99%). Never use eyeglass wipes or rubbing alcohol for cleaning. |

### 2.3 Personal protective equipment {#23-personal-protective-equipment}

#### 2.3.1 Mandatory PPE {#231-mandatory-ppe}

> **<i class="fas fa-ban" style="color: #d32f2f;"></i> DANGER** - _Wearing laser safety glasses is **MANDATORY** for anyone present in the room where the laser is operating._

**Laser safety glasses:**

| **CHARACTERISTIC**   | **REQUIREMENT**          |
| :------------------- | :----------------------- |
| Protection range     | 190 - 540 nm             |
| Optical density (OD) | 7+ minimum               |
| Applicable standard  | EN 207                   |
| Marking              | "D" for continuous laser |

<ul style="list-style-type: none;">
  <li><i class="fas fa-square-check" style="color: #4EBD7D;"></i> One pair of OD7+ glasses is supplied with the kit</li>

**Respiratory mask:**

- Recommended for prolonged work
- P2/P3 particulate filter minimum
- Activated carbon filter for organic vapors

#### 2.3.2 Clothing {#232-clothing}

**<i class="fas fa-square-check" style="color: #4EBD7D;"></i> RECOMMENDED:**

- Dark clothing (not white)
- Long sleeves in cotton or natural material
- Long hair tied back
- Closed shoes

**<i class="fas fa-xmark" style="color: #d32f2f;"></i> PROHIBITED:**

- Reflective jewelry (rings, watches, bracelets)
- Flammable synthetic clothing
- White and loose clothing

### 2.4 Safety devices {#24-safety-devices}

#### 2.4.1 Key switch {#241-key-switch}

**Characteristics:**

- Located on the PLH3D-CNC Adapter Pro
- Controls general power supply to the laser system
- The key must be removed and kept by the laser supervisor when the system is not in use

**Operation:**

1. **OFF position:** No laser power
2. **ON position:** The system can be armed

#### 2.4.2 Arming button {#242-arming-button}

**Characteristics:**

- Red button visible on the adapter
- Enables powering the laser after activating the key switch
- Double safety: key + arming required

**Operation:**

1. The key switch must be in the ON position
2. Press the arming button to activate the laser
3. The laser is now ready to receive commands

#### 2.4.3 Overheating protection {#243-overheating-protection}

The PLH3D-XT-50 module includes automatic thermal protection:

- **Cutoff temperature:** 45°C (113°F) at the diode housing level
- **Restart temperature:** 40°C (103°F)
- The laser shuts down automatically in case of overheating
- Operation resumes automatically once cooled

#### 2.4.4 Emergency stop {#244-emergency-stop}

The CNC machine emergency stop also cuts power to the laser:

- Immediate stop of all movements
- Laser control signal cutoff
- Manual reset required

## 3. TECHNICAL DESCRIPTION {#3-technical-description}

### 3.1 Overview {#31-overview}

The PLH3D-XT-50 laser module is a high-performance diode laser engraving and cutting system, integrable into MEKANIKA CNC mills via a magnetic docking station.

#### 3.1.1 Kit components {#311-kit-components}

| **COMPONENT**                       | **DESCRIPTION**                                |
| :---------------------------------- | :--------------------------------------------- |
| PLH3D-XT-50 laser head              | 6W optical laser module, 445nm                 |
| PLH3D-CNC Adapter Pro               | Control unit with key switch and arming button |
| Magnetic docking station            | Quick mount/dismount system                    |
| Connection cables                   | Adapter - laser and adapter - machine link     |
| OD7+ safety glasses                 | 190-540nm eye protection                       |
| HP compressed air nozzle (optional) | For high pressure air assistance               |

#### 3.1.2 Wiring diagram {#312-wiring-diagram}

**Main connections:**

- **Laser power:** Dedicated power supply → Adapter → Laser (19V DC)
- **Control signal:** CNC Machine → Adapter → Laser (5V PWM)
- **Emergency stop:** CNC Machine → Adapter (NC signal)

### 3.2 Technical specifications {#32-technical-specifications}

| **CHARACTERISTIC**           | **VALUE**                       |
| :--------------------------- | :------------------------------ |
| **OPTICAL**                  |                                 |

| **ELECTRICAL**               |                                 |

| **MECHANICAL**               |                                 |
| Dimensions                   | 40 × 55 × 140 mm                |
| Weight                       | 370 g                           |
| Mounting holes               | 4× M3, spacing 24 × 15 mm       |
| Working distance             | 60 mm (nominal)                 |
| **COOLING**                  |                                 |

| Sound level                  | 58 dBA                          |
| **ENVIRONMENT**              |                                 |
| Operating temperature        | 0-45°C (32-113°F)               |
| Thermal protection           | Cutoff at 45°C, resume at 40°C  |

### 3.3 Electrical inputs {#33-electrical-inputs}

**Pin description:**

| **PIN** | **FUNCTION**     | **DESCRIPTION**                     |
| :------ | :--------------- | :---------------------------------- |
| 1       | Mod Input #1 ANG | Analog or PWM input 0-5V            |
| 2       | Mod Input #2 PWM | PWM input 3-24V                     |
| 3       | Mod Input GND    | Common ground for modulation inputs |
| 4       | GND              | Power ground                        |
| 5       | Vcc              | 12-24V DC power supply              |

**PWM signal recommendations:**

- Recommended frequency: 5,000 - 10,000 Hz
- Higher frequencies produce better grayscale results
- Duty cycle percentage corresponds to power percentage

> **<i class="fas fa-info-circle" style="color: #2196f3;"></i> CAUTION** - Never connect Mod Input #1 and Mod Input #2 simultaneously.

#### 5.1.1 Safety checklist {#511-safety-checklist}

### 5.3 Working distance calibration {#53-working-distance-calibration}

The nominal working distance (WD) of the PLH3D-XT-50 is **60 mm** between the lower face of the laser head and the material.

> **<i class="fas fa-info-circle" style="color: #2196f3;"></i> IMPORTANT** - Precise calibration is necessary during first use and after any dismounting/remounting of the laser.

#### 5.3.1 Calibration procedure {#531-calibration-procedure}

**First pass - Coarse calibration:**

1. Position the laser 60 mm from the material
2. Engrave test lines at different heights: 55, 56, 57, 58, 59, 60, 61, 62, 63, 64, 65 mm
3. Identify the height where lines are finest
4. This height becomes the new reference distance

**Second pass - Fine calibration:**

1. Around the best height found, engrave lines in 0.1 mm steps
2. Example: if 59 mm was optimal, test 58.5 to 59.5 mm in 0.1 mm steps
3. Identify the optimal working distance

> **<i class="fas fa-info-circle" style="color: #2196f3;"></i> NOTE** - The XT-50 beam profile is always symmetrical.

| **SYMPTOM**            | **PROBABLE CAUSE**                              | **ACTION**                                          |
| :--------------------- | :---------------------------------------------- | :-------------------------------------------------- |
| **Visible flame**      | Material combustion                             | EMERGENCY → Immediate stop, extinguish if necessary |
| **Excessive smoke**    | Insufficient ventilation or unsuitable material | STOP → Check ventilation and material               |

| **Abnormal fan noise** | Obstruction or failure                          | STOP → Inspect fan                                  |

### 7.2 Lens cleaning {#72-lens-cleaning}

The front lens must be cleaned every 100 hours of operation.

**Product to use:**

- Isopropyl alcohol (≥ 99%)

**Procedure:**

1. Turn off laser and wait for cooling
2. Apply a small amount of IPA on a clean optical cloth
3. Gently clean the lens in circular motions
4. Allow to dry completely before use

> **<i class="fas fa-xmark" style="color: #d32f2f;"></i> NEVER USE:**
>
> - Eyeglass wipes (contain water and additives)
> - Rubbing alcohol (~70% IPA, contains water)
> - Household cleaning products

### 7.3 Reflective materials {#73-reflective-materials}

When working on highly reflective materials (anodized aluminum, stainless steel, titanium): **always tilt the material slightly (4-7°) to avoid back-reflections to the laser diode, which extends module life.**
